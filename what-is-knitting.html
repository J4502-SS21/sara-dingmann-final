What is Knitting?

History of Knitting

Knitting is the process of knotting one strand of yarn together to form a fabric. This was easier to do than other forms of creating fabric, such as weaving, because it required fewer and smaller tools. Knitting has been around for centuries, back as far as the 11th century. Many historians believe that knitting originated in the Middle East. Knitting came to American with European colonization.

Knitting became mechanized in the 1500s in France during the Industrial Revolution. However, it wasn’t until the 1800s that knitting moved to factories after the invention of a steam powered knitting machine. This is when knitting became more of a hobby and less of a necessity.

How is it differnet from crocheting?

So, what is the difference between knitting and crocheting? Both are ways of looping together yarn together to form a fabric. And they essentially are the same. Knitting creates more “v” like loops while crocheting creates loops that are more like knots.

As for which is better, that is up to personal preference. Crocheting is actually easier and faster than knitting. Since crocheting only requires one tool, it is a lot easier for people to pick up and cheaper to start. While knitting does take more time and costs more money because of the need for more tools, it can create more complicated designs.
